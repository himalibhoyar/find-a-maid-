Finding a maid, made easier with AI

A 6-year-old booking app for hiring domestic help, revisited with one goal: replace slow manual searching with a quick conversation. Same core flow — just a faster way through it.
Role
UI/UX Designer — Himali

The Problem

Booking help still felt like filling out a form

People need domestic help they can trust — but finding it usually means scrolling long lists and adjusting filters one at a time.

The question this redesign answers: what if booking felt like asking a friend, instead of filling out a form?
Who This Is For

Busy people, simple need

  • Meera — a working professional with two kids and little time to compare profiles by hand.
  • She doesn't think in filters ("2BHK, weekday mornings, ₹X"). She thinks in sentences: "I need someone for cooking and cleaning, mornings, weekdays."
  • That's the exact gap the AI assistant fills.
The Flow

Same journey, one new shortcut

The AI assistant sits next to the search bar, not instead of it. Anyone who prefers scrolling and filtering manually still can — both paths land on the same profile and booking screens.

Prioritization

Why the AI assistant, and not something else first

Before building, I scored the AI assistant against other candidate features using RICE — a simple way to compare how much a feature is worth building relative to its cost.

RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort. Higher score = better return for the work involved.
FeatureReachImpactConfidenceEffortScore
AI Chat Assistant8,000/mo380%4 wks480
Trust badges v28,000/mo290%2 wks720
Filter redesign only8,000/mo190%1 wk720
Post-booking chat3,000/mo260%3 wks120
Loyalty rewards2,000/mo150%2 wks50
→

Why AI won anyway: trust badges and filter tweaks score higher on paper because they're cheap. But they don't fix the actual problem — slow, manual searching. The AI assistant has the biggest impact on the one thing this redesign set out to solve, so it was worth the extra effort.

Scores estimated from typical engagement patterns for booking-style apps at this scale — not measured data from this specific product.
Reflection

What I'd tell a younger version of this project

Keep the structure that already works. Add only where it removes real effort. The AI layer here doesn't replace anything — it just gives people a faster way in, while the original path stays exactly where it was.
